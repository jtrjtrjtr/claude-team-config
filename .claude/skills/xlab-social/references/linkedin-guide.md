# LinkedIn Reference Guide for XLAB Company Page

## Post Formats & Dimensions

| Format | Specs | Use Case |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Single image | 1200 × 627 (1.91:1) or 1080 × 1080 (1:1) | Standard post with one visual |
| Carousel / Document | PDF upload, up to 300 pages, 1:1 or 4:3 | Multi-slide storytelling, higher engagement |
| Video | MP4, max 10 min, 1:1 or 16:9 | Event recaps, process videos |
| Text-only | No image | Rarely recommended for company pages |
| Article | Long-form on LinkedIn | Deep dives (rarely used for XLAB) |

**XLAB default recommendation:** Single image (1:1) for most posts. Document carousel for complex projects or case studies. Video when available.

## Company Page vs Personal Profile

This skill generates content for XLAB's **company page**. Key differences from personal profiles:

- Company pages have lower organic reach — content must earn engagement to spread
- The primary amplification mechanism is **employee reshares** — always note this to the user
- Tone is professional but not corporate — XLAB voice guide applies fully
- No first-person singular ("I") — use "we" or passive constructions
- The audience is: potential clients, industry peers, potential hires, partners

## Post Text Structure

### The "Fold" Problem

LinkedIn truncates company page posts after approximately **140–150 characters** (varies by device). Everything after that is hidden behind "...zobrazit více" / "...see more".

This means the **opening line is the most important line in the entire post**. It must be:
- Specific enough to be interesting
- Complete enough to make sense standalone
- Compelling enough to click "see more"

### Structure Template

```
[Opening line — specific, factual hook. Max 140 chars. Visible in feed.]

[Context — 1-2 sentences. What was the project, who was the client, what was the brief.]

[XLAB's role — 2-3 sentences. What we did, what technology, what approach.
This is where the professional audience wants detail.]

[Result — 1 sentence. Audience size, outcome, or a concrete observation.]

[Closing — optional. Forward-looking or acknowledgment. No CTA.]
```

### Length

- **Sweet spot:** 800–1,500 characters
- **Minimum:** 400 characters (shorter posts look unfinished on company pages)
- **Maximum:** 3,000 characters (LinkedIn hard limit; posts this long need strong content)
- Posts with 1,000–1,300 characters tend to perform best on company pages

### Opening Line Patterns (XLAB-approved)

| Pattern | Example CZ | Example EN |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Technology + scale | Videomapping na fasádě o rozloze 800 m² – šest projektorů, jedna noc. | Projection mapping on an 800 m² facade — six projectors, one night. |
| Challenge + solution | Jak propojit dvě pódia vzdálená 400 km v reálném čase? Holografickým teleportem. | How to connect two stages 400 km apart in real time? Holographic teleport. |
| Client + project | Pro Českou filharmonii jsme připravili videomapping na Rudolfinum. | For the Czech Philharmonic, we produced a projection mapping on the Rudolfinum. |
| Number-led | 12 projektorů, 40 metrů fasády, 8 minut obsahu – mapping pro Signal Festival. | 12 projectors, 40 metres of facade, 8 minutes of content — mapping for Signal Festival. |

Note: The "Challenge + solution" pattern uses a question, which is normally avoided in XLAB voice. On LinkedIn, a single factual question as an opener is acceptable if it's immediately answered — it functions as a structural device, not a rhetorical flourish.

### Opening Line Anti-Patterns

- ❌ "S potěšením oznamujeme..." / "We are pleased to announce..."
- ❌ "Jsme hrdí na..." / "We are proud of..."
- ❌ "Další skvělý projekt za námi." / "Another great project behind us."
- ❌ "V dnešní době, kdy..." / "In today's world, where..."
- ❌ Any sentence that could apply to any company in any industry

## Technology Detail Level

LinkedIn's professional audience expects more technical specificity than Instagram. Include:

- **Named technologies:** Disguise, Notch, Resolume, TouchDesigner, Unreal Engine, Ventuz — whatever was actually used
- **Hardware specs:** projector count and lumen rating, LED pitch, screen dimensions
- **Process details:** calibration approach, content pipeline, real-time vs pre-rendered
- **Team scope:** number of people on-site, disciplines involved (but not names unless relevant)

This is not about showing off — it's about being precise. The xlab-content principle applies: say what we do, concretely and clearly.

## Hashtags on LinkedIn

**Default: no hashtags.** XLAB's LinkedIn posts do not use hashtags by default.

If the user specifically requests hashtags:
- Maximum 3–5
- Place at the very end, separated by a line break
- Use only broad industry tags: #ProjectionMapping #EventTech #ImmersiveExperience
- Never use branded hashtags on LinkedIn (they don't function the same way as on Instagram)

## Tagging

- **Client company:** Tag when the relationship is public and it adds value. Always ask the user.
- **Partner companies:** Tag when they contributed significantly (AV suppliers, creative collaborators)
- **Individuals:** Tag specific people (speakers, artists) only with user confirmation
- **Tagging increases reach** — LinkedIn's algorithm favors posts that generate cross-company engagement

## Visual Content

### Single Image
- 1:1 (square) or 1200×627 (landscape) — both work well
- High-quality production photo preferred over designed graphics
- No heavy text overlays — LinkedIn previews are small on mobile
- XLAB brand elements (logo, X symbol) subtle or absent — the post already comes from the XLAB page

### Document Carousel
- Upload as PDF
- Recommended: 4:3 or 1:1 slides
- Design: clean, XLAB brand guidelines (xlab-brand skill)
- Content: project case study, technology breakdown, process walkthrough
- 5–10 slides maximum for engagement
- First slide = cover with project name (same principles as Instagram cover)
- Last slide = summary or credit slide

### Video
- 1:1 or 16:9
- Subtitles always — most LinkedIn users watch without sound
- 30–90 seconds for event recaps
- Thumbnail matters — LinkedIn auto-selects, but a custom thumbnail improves CTR

## Content Calendar Notes

- **Posting frequency for company pages:** 2–4 times per week is optimal. Less than once a week = page looks inactive. More than daily = diminishing returns.
- **Best times for B2B in Europe:** Tuesday–Thursday, 8:00–10:00 or 12:00–13:00 CET
- **Post-event timing:** Ideal to post within 48 hours while the event is still fresh. A well-crafted post a week later is better than a rushed post the same day.

## Employee Reshare Strategy

Company page reach is limited. The primary amplification mechanism is employee reshares.

When generating a LinkedIn post, note to the user:
- The post should be written so it's easy for XLAB team members to reshare
- Reshared posts with a personal comment from the employee perform 3–5× better
- Consider: could this post benefit from a parallel personal post by a team member (CEO, creative director) offering their perspective?

This skill does not generate personal profile posts — but it flags when a personal post would be valuable.
