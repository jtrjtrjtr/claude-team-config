# XLAB Events — Creative Preferences

## Version: 0.1 (Initial)
## Last updated: 2026-03-08

This file captures creative patterns and preferences that emerge from real project work. It is not a rulebook — it is a living memory of what works, what the team prefers, and what to avoid.

### How to Use
- Check this file when developing creative concepts
- Patterns here are starting points, not constraints
- If a brief demands something different, break the pattern and note why
- Update this file after each project based on feedback

---

## Concept Naming
- Every proposal has a named concept (1–3 words)
- Name should be evocative, not descriptive
- Good: "Anima Montis", "Mirror", "Universitas"
- Avoid: "Interactive Tech Event", "Digital Experience Show"

## Visual World
- XLAB gravitates toward organic over geometric (mycelia, light, water, nature)
- Dark environments with controlled light are preferred over bright/white spaces
- Physical materiality matters — even digital installations benefit from tangible elements
- Semi-transparent mirror foil is a proven design device (depth illusion, technology concealment)

## Dramaturgical Preferences
- Slow build > sudden reveal. The "wow" moment is more powerful after quiet preparation.
- Sound and atmosphere are valued equally to visuals. Best pavilions at Expo were not the flashiest — they had the best vibe and music.
- Delayed gratification in interactive elements (child draws → creature appears in next cycle) over instant feedback
- Transition spaces (tunnels, corridors, thresholds) are dramaturgical opportunities, not dead zones

## Technology Approach
- Always present technology variants with clear recommendation and transparent pricing logic
- Redundancy is non-negotiable for permanent installations (hot-standby backup)
- Prefer laser projectors over lamp-based (lifetime, maintenance, color consistency)
- Prefer software-defined show control (schedulable, remotely manageable) over manual
- Grey projection surfaces for domes (not white — reduces parazitic light scatter)

## Proposal Style
- Lead with the experience, reveal technology as enabler
- Named team members with roles and relevant references
- References curated to match the brief, not generic portfolio
- Attendee journey is always included (spatial or temporal)
- Pricing presented with variants and clear recommendation

## Content Production
- Unreal Engine preferred for realistic immersive environments
- TouchDesigner preferred for interactive layers and real-time processing
- 360° timelapse from client-specific locations adds unique value to installations
- Generative/procedural content for ambient layers (sustainable, evolving, low-maintenance)

## What to Avoid
- "Cutting-edge" and "state-of-the-art" — banned words
- Technology-first pitches (specs before experience)
- Generic portfolio pages without brief-specific curation
- Over-promising interactivity that degrades under load
- Commitment to specific dates or prices in concepts (use "subject to confirmation")
- **Negative framing — absolute ban.** Never "X, not Y" / "this is not X, this is Y" / "unlike X, we..." — always state the positive directly. This applies everywhere: proposals, concepts, emails, internal docs.
- **Content-writing register in creative contexts.** When writing creative concepts, experience narratives, or spatial storytelling, use vivid atmospheric language. Do not default to the factual xlab-content Communication Mode register. Creative texts should make the reader feel the experience. If in doubt, ask the user which register to use.
