# Engagement Architecture — 360° Attendee Engagement

## Purpose

Modern events don't start when the doors open and end when the lights go off. This framework defines how to design engagement across the full attendee lifecycle — before, during, and after the event.

## Pre-Event Engagement

### Why It Matters
Attendees who are engaged before arrival are 3× more likely to participate actively on-site. Pre-event engagement converts passive attendees into active participants.

### Touchpoints

**Invitation design:** The first creative touchpoint. Should establish the visual world and tone. For experiential events, the invitation can be an experience itself (AR, interactive web, physical object).

**Registration experience:** Functional but branded. Opportunity to capture attendee preferences, dietary requirements, session choices — and to personalize the on-site experience based on responses.

**Pre-event content:** Teaser videos, speaker introductions, agenda previews, city guides for out-of-town attendees. Builds anticipation without revealing everything.

**Community activation:** For conferences — enable networking before arrival. Attendee list (opt-in), discussion forum, matchmaking. For launches — exclusive pre-briefing for key stakeholders.

**Logistics communication:** Travel, accommodation, dress code, accessibility info. The unsexy but critical hygiene layer. Poor logistics communication undermines all creative engagement.

### XLAB Application
Include pre-event engagement in proposals when: budget allows, event is 200+ attendees, or client explicitly values digital touchpoints. For smaller events, at minimum recommend: branded invitation + logistics email + teaser visual.

## During-Event Engagement

### The Attention Economy Problem
Attendees have smartphones. Every moment they check their phone instead of looking up, the event loses. The solution is not "ban phones" — it's "make the event more interesting than the phone."

### Engagement Design Principles

**Variety of input modes:** Alternate between watching, doing, talking, moving, eating, creating. No single mode should last more than 45 minutes without a shift.

**Opt-in depth:** Provide layers of engagement depth. The casual attendee gets a good experience. The curious attendee discovers more. The enthusiast is rewarded with exclusive access or deeper interaction.

**Social proof mechanics:** Make engagement visible. When people see others interacting with an installation, they're more likely to try it. Design for visible queuing, visible participation, visible outcomes.

**The Photo Moment:** Design at least one moment that is irresistibly photogenic. This is not vanity — it's the event's organic marketing channel. The photo moment needs: good lighting, clear visual composition, and a reason for the attendee to be in the frame (not just photographing, but being part of it).

### Engagement Formats Toolkit

**For conferences:**
- Live polling / Q&A via mobile (standard, but must be well-moderated)
- Session-to-session gamification (completionism rewards)
- Networking matchmaking (algorithm-based meeting suggestions)
- Interactive expo with hands-on demos (not just brochure stands)
- Expert roundtables (10 people, 20 minutes, rotating)

**For launches / experiential:**
- Guided journey with personal narrative (dedicated host, branded token)
- Co-creation stations (draw, build, design something that becomes part of the event)
- Progressive reveal (information unlocks as you move through zones)
- Personalization (your name on the installation, your data visualized, your photo transformed)
- Sensory surprise (scent, temperature change, tactile material, unexpected sound)

**For brand activations:**
- Social sharing mechanic (Boomerang-ready moment, branded filter, instant print)
- Challenge or game (simple, 30-second, rewarding)
- Product integration (the brand's product as part of the interactive experience, not just displayed)
- User-generated content wall (social feed displayed live)

**For installations:**
- Self-paced discovery (no forced path, but designed flow)
- Repeatable experience (different each time due to generative or responsive content)
- Souvenir generation (printed, digital, or physical takeaway)
- Hidden rewards for attentive visitors (easter eggs, details visible only up close)

## Post-Event Engagement

### The Forgetting Curve
Without reinforcement, attendees forget 70% of event content within 48 hours. Post-event engagement is not a "nice to have" — it's what converts a moment into a memory.

### Touchpoints

**Immediate (0–24 hours):**
- Thank-you message (personalized if possible)
- Photo/video highlights (fast turnaround, shareable format)
- Digital souvenir delivery (if applicable)
- Social media recap (tagging attendees where appropriate)

**Short-term (1–7 days):**
- Event content library (session recordings, presentations, resources)
- Attendee survey (brief, focused, with incentive)
- Engagement report for client (metrics, highlights, recommendations)

**Long-term (1–3 months):**
- Follow-up content series (extending event themes)
- Reactivation for next event (save the date, early access)
- Case study production (with client approval)

### XLAB Application
Include post-event deliverables in proposals when relevant: photo/video documentation, engagement report, content library setup. These add value to the proposal and demonstrate thinking beyond the event day itself.

## Measurement by Phase

| Phase | What to measure | How |
|-------|----------------|-----|
| Pre-event | Registration rate, email open/click, pre-event content views | Analytics |
| During | Attendance, dwell time by zone, interaction count, social mentions | Sensors, analytics, social listening |
| Post | Survey score, content downloads, social reach, media coverage | Survey tool, analytics, clipping |

## Including Engagement in Proposals

**Minimum:** Attendee journey map showing key engagement moments during the event.

**Standard:** Journey map + 2–3 specific engagement mechanics described in detail + photo moment design.

**Premium:** Full 360° engagement plan across pre/during/post + measurement framework + content strategy.

Match the level to the brief. A €50k event doesn't need a 360° engagement plan. A €500k+ event does.
