# Event Types — Classification & Approach Guide

## Purpose

Not every event requires the same proposal intensity. This guide defines event types XLAB encounters and the appropriate response for each. Classification drives: creative scope, proposal structure, team composition, and pricing approach.

## Type Matrix

### 1. Corporate Conference
**What it is:** Multi-session knowledge-sharing event. Keynotes, breakouts, networking, expo. Usually 200–2000 attendees, 1–3 days.
**Client profile:** Corporate marketing or events team, structured procurement.
**Creative scope:** Enhanced — creativity lives in stage design, attendee experience design, evening program, and technology integration. The agenda is usually pre-set.
**XLAB role:** AV + stage + design + content production. Sometimes full production, sometimes tech partner to lead agency.
**Proposal focus:** Execution excellence, venue optimization, AV spec detail, team depth, references.
**Hero deliverable:** Plenary stage design + attendee journey + detailed AV specification.
**Pricing model:** Detailed line-item (xlab-pricing Standard mode).
**Examples from XLAB history:** Visa University CEE Learning Festival.

### 2. Brand Activation
**What it is:** Experiential marketing installation at a festival, event, or public space. Goal: brand awareness, engagement, shareability.
**Client profile:** Brand marketing team, often through agency.
**Creative scope:** Full creative — the concept IS the product.
**XLAB role:** Concept, design, technology, content production, onsite.
**Proposal focus:** The idea. Visual impact. How it generates attention and social sharing. Technical feasibility.
**Hero deliverable:** Visual concept + 3D renders + visitor flow + social sharing mechanic.
**Pricing model:** Typically project-based (xlab-pricing Freeform or Set/celek for creative).
**Examples from XLAB history:** Pilsner Urquell Inside the Craft (KVIFF/Grape).

### 3. Experiential Launch
**What it is:** Product, brand, or venue launch designed as an immersive journey. High-touch, curated guest list, storytelling-driven.
**Client profile:** Corporate comms, investor relations, or brand team. VIP audience.
**Creative scope:** Full creative — the space itself tells the story.
**XLAB role:** Concept-to-execution. Often includes custom build, spatial design, technology integration, content.
**Proposal focus:** Narrative arc, zone design, attendee journey (minute by minute), wow moment, logistics for VIP handling.
**Hero deliverable:** Zone map + attendee journey + narrative storyboard + production timeline.
**Pricing model:** Detailed with variants (standard vs. premium build).
**Examples from XLAB history:** MHAP Arena Launch (with IGLU custom build, 7 thematic zones).

### 4. Immersive Installation (Permanent / Semi-permanent)
**What it is:** Standalone attraction or exhibition using projection, LED, interactive technology. Operates on ticketing model.
**Client profile:** Venue operators, museums, tourist attractions, cultural institutions.
**Creative scope:** Full creative + technical design. Content production is a major deliverable.
**XLAB role:** End-to-end: concept, technical architecture, content production, installation, training.
**Proposal focus:** Experience narrative, technical specification, content production plan, operational model, ROI projection.
**Hero deliverable:** Creative concept + technical architecture document + content production timeline + budget with variants.
**Pricing model:** Multi-sheet: hardware, content production, installation, licensing/maintenance.
**Examples from XLAB history:** Bachledka Anima Montis (dome projection + interactive stations).

### 5. Gala / Ceremony
**What it is:** Formal seated event with program, entertainment, dining. Often awards, anniversaries, celebrations.
**Client profile:** Corporate events team or agency.
**Creative scope:** Enhanced — concept drives the visual world, but format is traditional.
**XLAB role:** Stage design, AV, lighting design, show direction, content production.
**Proposal focus:** Visual concept, run-of-show, entertainment curation, technical rider.
**Hero deliverable:** Stage design mockup + run-of-show with cue-by-cue + lighting concept.
**Pricing model:** Standard line-item.
**Examples from XLAB history:** Česká spořitelna 200 years Rudolfinum gala.

### 6. Cultural / Art Event
**What it is:** Projection mapping, light festival, art installation in cultural context. Public audience.
**Client profile:** Municipal authorities, cultural institutions, festivals.
**Creative scope:** Full creative — artistic freedom highest here.
**XLAB role:** Creative direction, content production, technical production, show direction.
**Proposal focus:** Artistic vision, site-specific concept, community impact, technical feasibility.
**Hero deliverable:** Site-specific creative concept + technical plot + content storyboard.
**Pricing model:** Project-based.
**Examples from XLAB history:** Signal Festival, Křižíkova Fontána, SNP/Orlík mapping.

### 7. Hybrid / Virtual Event
**What it is:** Event with significant online component — livestream, interactive virtual platform, hybrid audience engagement.
**Client profile:** Corporate events with distributed teams or global reach.
**Creative scope:** Standard to Enhanced — platform and content design matter more than physical space.
**XLAB role:** Virtual production, streaming setup, platform integration, content.
**Proposal focus:** Platform recommendation, production quality, engagement tools, technical reliability.
**Hero deliverable:** Platform architecture + production setup + engagement mechanic design.
**Pricing model:** Standard line-item + platform licensing.

### 8. Agency RFP (Retainer / Roster)
**What it is:** Not a single event but a selection process for ongoing partnership. Client selects agency for a period (typically 1–3 years).
**Client profile:** Large corporation with procurement-driven process.
**Creative scope:** N/A — this is about capability, not a specific concept.
**XLAB role:** Present as capable, innovative, reliable partner with competitive pricing.
**Proposal focus:** Company profile, capabilities, team, references, rate card, case studies, process description.
**Hero deliverable:** Company presentation + pricing sheet + 2–3 detailed case studies.
**Pricing model:** Rate card (T&M).
**Examples from XLAB history:** SAP Czech Republic RFP.

## How to Use This Classification

1. When a brief arrives, classify it using the types above.
2. If a brief spans multiple types (e.g., conference with experiential launch element), identify the PRIMARY type (drives proposal structure) and SECONDARY type (addressed as a module within the proposal).
3. Use the "Proposal focus" and "Hero deliverable" fields to prioritize what to produce.
4. Match the creative scope to avoid over-investing (standard conference) or under-delivering (full creative pitch).
