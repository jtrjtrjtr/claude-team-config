# XLAB Past Projects — Reference Extracts

This file contains structured extracts from real XLAB briefs and proposals. Use these as reference patterns — not templates to copy. Each entry captures the brief essence, XLAB's approach, and transferable learnings.

## Project 1: Česká spořitelna — 200 Years Anniversary (2025)

**Type:** Full Creative (Gala + Mass Employee Event)
**Client:** Česká spořitelna / Erste Group
**Brief source:** Formal RFP via Erste Group Procurement

### Brief Essence
Two separate events celebrating 200 years: a 1,000-person VIP gala at Rudolfinum (€350k budget) and a 12,500-person all-employee celebration at O2 Arena (€2M budget). Campaign hashtag #silnějšíbudoucnost. Czech Philharmonic mandatory at gala. Creative brief was open.

### XLAB Approach (with partner New Wave)
- Throughline concept: Mirror / Reflection — connecting tradition with future
- Attendee journey mapped for both events
- Moodboards and run-of-show included
- AI avatar proposed as event guide
- Digital time capsule as engagement mechanic
- Entertainer combination proposals with specific names
- Detailed team listing with roles and personal references

### Transferable Patterns
- For dual-event briefs: maintain one concept across both, but adapt scale and tone
- Open creative briefs deserve bold concepts, not safe ones
- AI avatar as guide/host is a proven XLAB differentiator for galas and corporate events
- Including specific entertainment names shows industry network depth
- The "one concept, two executions" approach works well for anniversary events

## Project 2: Visa University — CEE Learning Festival (2026)

**Type:** Enhanced Conference
**Client:** Visa
**Brief source:** 15-slide brand brief, structured but focused

### Brief Essence
2.5-day event in Budapest for 400 C-level attendees from CEE. Budget €1.5M. Agenda pre-defined (Day 0–3). Strong emphasis on visual cleanliness, seamless execution, and technological solutions. Venue requirements and AV specs provided.

### XLAB Approach
- Throughline concept: Universitas / Community — visual system based on pillars
- Venue recommendation: Corinthia Budapest as all-in-one solution
- Entry installation as first-impression moment
- Detailed zone design: hospitality desk, plenary, breakouts, expo
- Specific team with roles and Visa ecosystem references
- Gala designed as offsite separate experience

### Transferable Patterns
- For structured conferences: creativity lives in design language, entry moment, and evening program — not in rewriting the agenda
- Venue recommendation adds value even when not requested
- C-level audience requires: minimal friction, invisible technology, premium materials
- "Pillar" visual system is a proven device for multi-track conferences (each track = visual identity)
- References from within the client's industry/ecosystem carry more weight than generic references

## Project 3: MHAP — Multifunctional Hockey Arena Pardubice Launch (2026)

**Type:** Experiential Launch
**Client:** MHAP (arena development project)
**Brief source:** 24-slide detailed brief with scoring criteria

### Brief Essence
Launch event for a new arena brand concept. 500 VIP attendees (investors, politicians, promoters). Budget €1–1.5M. Bilingual CZ/EN. Storytelling focused on building trust in the project. Evaluation: 25% program+tech, 25% creativity, 15% service+team, 25% budget, 10% credentials.

### XLAB Approach (with partner Event Arena)
- Custom venue concept: IGLU structure 40×70m as the event itself
- 7 thematic zones as immersive journey, not sequential presentations
- Zone examples: mapping table for location concept, LED diorama for architecture, holographic display for digital solutions, VR experience
- 3-day tiered access: Ultra VIP → VIP → Public
- Rotation logic: 5 groups of 20, color-coded, dedicated hosts
- Closed-door meeting rooms integrated into the journey

### Transferable Patterns
- For launch events: the venue IS the message — custom builds justify themselves
- Zone-based journey is more memorable than stage presentation for VIP audiences
- Rotation logic with dedicated hosts scales VIP experience to 500+ people
- Tiered access (Ultra VIP → VIP → Public) creates exclusivity and extends content ROI
- When scoring criteria are published, explicitly address each criterion in the proposal
- Custom physical build (IGLU) demonstrates commitment and differentiates from standard proposals

## Project 4: Bachledka — Anima Montis (2026)

**Type:** Immersive Installation (Permanent)
**Client:** Bachledka Ski and Sun (Slovakia)
**Brief source:** Email inquiry + phone call + strategy PDF

### Brief Essence
Summer attraction in two connected domes (6m and 15m diameter) with tunnel. Target: families, 60,000+ visitors/season, ticket price €6–10. Initial theme suggestion: bees/insects. Budget ~€100k for technology. Opening: June 15, 2026.

### XLAB Approach
- Creative concept: Anima Montis (Soul of the Mountain) — mycelium as nervous system of the landscape
- Abandoned client's initial bee theme in favor of stronger, more universal narrative
- Three-zone spatial dramaturgy: Signal (small dome) → Transfer (tunnel) → Immersion (large dome)
- 7-minute dome show: one accelerated year of forest life, from bug's perspective
- Children's interactive workshop: draw a creature → it appears in the dome projection
- Ambient layer: real 360° timelapse footage from hard-to-reach locations
- Mycelium as visual identity transferable to wayfinding and branding
- Three projector variants with transparent recommendation logic
- Separate technical architecture document with full specs

### Transferable Patterns
- For installations: don't accept the client's first theme idea if a better one exists — earn trust by showing creative vision
- Mycelium-as-visual-system is a masterclass in "one concept, many applications"
- The "delayed gratification" mechanic (child draws, creature appears later) is more engaging than instant
- Ambient content (timelapse from inaccessible places) adds value without heavy production
- Always present technology variants with clear recommendation and rationale
- Separate creative concept and technical document is the right approach for installations
- Preliminary Study as a paid first phase de-risks the project for both sides

## Project 5: Pilsner Urquell — Inside the Craft (2026)

**Type:** Brand Activation (Festival Installation)
**Client:** Pilsner Urquell (via agency)
**Brief source:** Integrated brand brief (structured marketing document)

### Brief Essence
Interactive visual installation for festival brand zones (KVIFF, Grape Festival). Budget €43k including LED technology purchase, content, and setup. Campaign platform: "Umění neměnit" (The Art of Not Changing). Goal: landmark installation that attracts attention, invites photography/sharing, and communicates brand values in the background.

### XLAB Approach
- Physical form: circular sculpture inspired by the bottom of a beer glass
- LED screen behind semi-transparent mirror foil — viewer perceives looking through glass, not watching a screen
- Content combines: live camera feed, abstract loops, brand motifs — unified through glass-optics visual language (kaleidoscope, refraction, distortion)
- Two venue adaptations: KVIFF (integrated into tent architecture) and Grape (freestanding object in open field)
- Modular and scalable — same visual principle, different physical form
- Interactive extension possible via QR/mobile for visitor participation
- Reference photo of mirror foil from ČS 200 years gala (cross-selling past work)

### Transferable Patterns
- For brand activations: the object IS the installation — physical form must be photogenic and iconic before any content plays
- Semi-transparent mirror as design device creates depth illusion and hides technology
- Festival installations must work in two modes: with and without content (the physical form alone must attract)
- Same concept adapting to different venues demonstrates versatility and long-term value
- Budget-conscious projects still need strong creative concept — "affordable" does not mean "generic"
- Cross-referencing own past work as proof of concept (mirror foil photo) is effective

## Project 6: SAP Czech Republic — Event Agency RFP (2025)

**Type:** Agency RFP (Retainer)
**Brief source:** Formal procurement RFP via SAP Ariba

### Brief Essence
Selection of event agency for ongoing partnership (3-year term, 2025–2028). Events range from 15-person workshops to 1,000-person conferences. Physical, hybrid, and virtual. Requires: project management, event coordination, design/creation, digital marketing, communication. Response via SAP Ariba platform only.

### Transferable Patterns
- For agency RFPs: respond to the format they require — don't impose your own
- Rate card / T&M pricing must be competitive for 3-year commitment
- Knowledge of the client's industry and ecosystem is a stated requirement
- GDPR compliance, sustainability, virtual event capabilities are table stakes
- Company presentation needs to cover: capability breadth, local network, team depth, case studies
- This type of RFP is won on reliability, pricing, and proven track record — not creative vision

## Cross-Project Patterns

1. **Every XLAB proposal has a named concept** — Mirror, Universitas, SPACE, Anima Montis, Inside the Craft. The concept is the organizing principle.

2. **Proposals are in English** for corporate and international clients, even for Czech-based projects.

3. **Team is always named** with specific roles and relevant experience.

4. **References are curated** to match the brief — always specifically selected for the context.

5. **Technology is presented as enabler**, with clear specs but in service of the experience narrative.

6. **XLAB always proposes more than asked** — not scope creep, but vision. The brief asks for AV; XLAB proposes an experience.

7. **Partners are integrated transparently** — Event Arena, New Wave, WOW Factor Hungary appear as named collaborators with defined roles.
